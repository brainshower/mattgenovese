Strategic Technology Community Engagement

An On-site Course for Chamber of Commerce Leadership

Overview

Is your Chamber of Commerce serving the unique needs of your technology industry?

Technologists, including engineers, software developers, and information technology professionals are among the highest wage-earners for most cities. See the salary statistics for yourself. The professionals, employers, and educational institutions that make up your technology industry collectively drive innovation and local prosperity.

Your Chamber of Commerce is in an excellent position to serve the unique needs inherent to that ecosystem, and in turn create value and drive economic growth for your region.

Learn the strategies to create value and foster growth in your local technology industry based on the unique model implemented and refined by Matt Genovese through the Austin, Texas-based organization he founded, Door64.

What is Door64?

Matt Genovese founded Door64 in 2007 and within five years grew it to become Austin’s largest networked group of technologists with 30,000 members. Door64 has designed and produced over 100 events from networking happy hours, job fairs, conferences, and other targeted engagements tied to specific results that directly benefit the Austin-area technology community. Countless technology professionals have found jobs, employers found their critical tech hires, companies found new business leads, and new employers arrived and grew in Austin through this unique and focused approach to technology community engagement.

What Your Chamber of Commerce Will Learn

  • How to create events that attract technology employers to your city
  • How to attract and retain local technology talent
  • Detailed examples of proven, successful, revenue-generating events that serve the technology community
  • How to accelerate the technical recruiting efforts of employers through activities uniquely available to the Chamber
  • Tested online tactics for growing your following and awareness in the local technology community
  • How to avoid pitfalls that will dilute your ability to deliver value to your technology community
And finally, do all this while generating new revenue for your Chamber!
