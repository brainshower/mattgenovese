About

Engineering, in many shapes and forms.

Community

I founded Door64 in 2007 with a goal of serving the high-tech demographic in Austin, Texas through targeted initiatives and events. Grew Door64 to 30,000 members, and produced over 100 events from networking happy hours, job fairs, conferences, and other targeted engagements tied to specific career or educational results that directly benefit the Austin-area technology community. Countless technology professionals have found jobs, employers found their critical tech hires, companies found new business leads, and new employers arrived and grew in Austin through this unique and focused approach to community engagement.

Education

I am passionate about technology education, especially in terms of continuing education opportunities for mid-career professionals that address the critical needs of local industry. I believe that closing the education-industry feedback loop is essential to the creation of customized (degree or non-degree) programs, leading to a more robust, adaptive, and well-connected technology ecosystem in a local community.

Software

Started programming on the Commodore VIC-20 at 8 years old. Taught myself BASIC, then 68XX assembly by sixth grade, and moved on to Pascal by high school. These days, I'm fluent in C, C++, Java, PHP, Perl, and more. I started using Linux in the 90's (kernel 1.3).

Web Development

I've been a Drupal developer since 2007, and I'm a big fan of the platform. I am especially good at understanding the problem, and creating an 80% solution using existing Drupal modules, and developing the other custom 20% as needed. These days I'm also a fan of Bootstrap (used for this site).

Semiconductors

Spent twelve years in the semiconductor industry both as a Product Engineer and a Verification Engineer, focusing on PowerPC-based SoC's. In terms of functional verification, I specialized in test bench methodology and development.
